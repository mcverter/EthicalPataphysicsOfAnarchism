    {% trans "Mathemeticians before Euclid studies geometrical shapes. Egyptians and Babylonians were able to understand and employ important properties of right angle triangles and other figures and employ them in their architqechture. The first Greek philosopher Thales developed an important theorem about triangles and parallel lines three centuries before Euclid. Pythagoras taught the his famous theorem about right angle triangles around the same time." %}

    {% trans "Euclid's most important contribution to mathematics and to philophy as well is his usage of proof. The Elements does not merely enumerate a set of interesitng propositoins about shapes. It develops a formal system of logic that enunciate a small set of axioms that will be taken to be self-evident and then derives a set of theorems that follow from these axioms." %}

    {% trans "This formalization of mathematics greatly influenced mathemetmatics of the 20th century as well as phenomenology. Hilbert propososed that mathematics was something that could broken down into a set of axioms and theorems. Russell and Whitehead attempted to do ground arithmetic and all of mathematics in the axioms of set theory in their 'Principia Mathematica'. However, Godel demonstrated that any system of axioms is necessarily incomplete and that such attempts were doomed to failure. Husserl, a colleague of Hilbert's at GÃ¶ttingen University, was once a mathematician himself. THroughout his work he discusses 'formal logic' which can be understood as an adoption of Hilbert's notion of axiomitaztion. This sysemization was very influential on Derrdia, whose earliest formulations of what would come to known as 'deconstruciton' made explicit use of Godel's incompleteness theorem and whose first book was an introduction on Husserl's 'The Origin of Geometry', a meditation on Euclid." %}

    Geometry without Number

    {% trans "First of all, Euclid's geometrical investigations have no concept of number and they do not need any. All of the lines, circles, and various shapes can be constructed with a compass and a straight edge." %}

    {% trans "Mathematical operations are defined through geometry, not calculation. Addition can be understood as as the juxtaposition of line segments. Multiplication is understood as the area of a square with sides of given lengths. Fractions and division are understood with similar geometrical constructions." %}

  • {% trans "Non-Euclidean Geometry" %}

    {% trans "Gauss What is curvature? In high school, we learn about a related subject: conic sections such as parabolas and hyperbolas and ellipses. This is different but related to the notion of curvature." %}

    {% trans "Gauss differentiated between two types of curvature: The intrinsic curvature of the graph itself and the external curvature of the space in which the object is embedded." %}

    {% trans "For example, let's draw a picture of a smiley face on a piece of paper, the canonical example of a flat surface. Now. let's roll that paper up into a cylinder. The space any two points on that piece of the paper even though you have rolled it up. We therefore say that a cylinder is also flat." %}

    {% trans "Conversely, let's say that we draw the smiley face on a balloon. Then let's pop that baloon and flatten it out. We will never be able to flatten out all of the curves of that deflated balloon to a picture similar to the one we had on the piece of paper. A balloon has different intrinsic curvature than the paper. Whereas the paper has zero curvature, a spherical ballon would have a curvature inversely proportional to the square of its radius." %}

    {% trans "Bernard Riemann" %}

    {% trans "Around the time that Gauss was working on curvature, thinkers such as Bolyai and Lobachevsky began to consider the nature of non-Euclidean geometry." %}

    {% trans "Non-Euclidean geometry is a direct challenge to Euclid's fifth postulate, which (in summary) claims that parallel lines never meet." %}

    {% trans "Even before these 19th century mathematicians, others who geometers had noted that Euclid's assrtion of the parallel postulate was equivalent to his assertions about right angles and right triangles, that straight lines have the angular measure equivallent to two right triangles, that there exist triangles with the same angles but different side lengths, that the sum of all angles in any triangle is equivalent to two right triangles." %}

    {% trans "Concern over non-Euclidean geometry was a practical necessity for naviators: because the earth is a globe, Euclid's rules for straight lines and right angles did not map correctly to their navigational charts. Geometers have to create maps with different types of distortion to express relations between spaces, to balance between the size of a land mass and its relative distance to other landmasses." %}

    {% trans "Bolyai and Lobachevsky's hyperbolic space demonstrated that spaces which did not follow Euclid's axioms were internally consistent" %}

    {% trans "The important implication of this nnon-euclidean geometry is that curved space is non-homogenous. The distance to the next point is contingent upon your location in the space. For example, let's say you are at the bottom a hill and you would have to go 1000 meters to get to the other side of the hill. But instead you climb up the hill. When you are at some height, you find that it would you would only have to go 800 meters to get to the other side of the hill. Finally, when you are the top, the two sides of the hill conicide and you are effectively zero meters from the opposite side of the hill." %}

    {% trans "Bernard Riemann provided a useful way to understand this analytically with the idea of n-ply manifold, a space of n-dimensions or a function of n variables. " %}

    {% trans "He reasoned that, although the space as a whole is curved, one could chop it up into tiny parts that looked like flat surfaces. By summing up these tiny intervals of locally flat space, one could calculte the lengths of the entire curved graph." %}

    {% trans "The key concepts here are local flatness and global curvature. " %}

    {% trans "Globally objects are not laid out in a right-angle grid structure, but they still appear locally as if they are flat." %}

    {% trans "The idea of chopping things up into their tiny parts and then summing them up is an idea from calculus." %}

    {% trans "Thus Riemann employs the idea of a geodisc, which defines the distance of the neighbors and defines integratoin over these geodiscs. " %}

    {% trans "This idea is a effectively a combination of projective geometry and analytic geometry. Distance is understood in terms of relative position, as in projective goemetry, but it can also be calculated as in analytic geometry." %}
