{% trans "Metaphoric Transformations of GENÈSES" %}

{% trans "Let us examine this topic by revisiting the notion of the Genre Set" %} {% genre_btn_collapse "GENÈSES" %} {% trans ", a genre set that encompasses the words 'birth' and 'death'. How do these two words function in the narrative described by Levinas. The narrative plot line that circles through Totality and Infinity is one of genesis: the story of how, after a certain death, out of nothing, someone is born, lives and then dies, but is resurrected in a new birth. But this narrative is laid out as a story of Genesis, borrowing motifs taken from the the Bible and the Greeks and thus tells a coming-of-age story as a series of steps that conform to these patriarchal mythologies. This story describes how one is born into a womb-like or infantile elemental state, oceanic and womblike, then recollects himself as an individual by marrying himself to a woman and positioning himself in a house, from which he performs the activity of labor that produces works and wastes, objects that are alienated from him until he as he ages and deteriorates, drifting drifts off towards a the unfathomable not-yet of death. Yet he is saved from death by intercourse with this same feminine, and becomes the father of a child who is resurrected as a brother among brothers. " %}
