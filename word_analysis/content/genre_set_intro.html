Our invocation of parallelism and homology are taken from the metaphors taken from analytic geometry. specifically projective and non-Euclidean geometry. These fields are decribed further in the section below. Without yet explaining the implications of the term 'curvature', we would like to assert that Levinas's terminology is best understood as a series of rotational transformations of a specific and enumerable set of genres, such as those of GENESE and MATHEMATICS

Levinas spells out a clear basis for this rotational movement as the structure of Totality and INfinity. The two central sections of the book, Interiority and Economy and Exteriority and the Face develop metaphors which are polar opposites of each other. Most obviously, 'inertioirity' and 'exterioirity' move in opposite directions to each other along a line of extension or involution.

Clockwise Rotations of Metaphors

The rotations discussed above are full 180 degree rotatoins from each other, but we would argue that there are other rotational symmetries within the text of TOtality and Infinity. Not only are these books divided into sections, but each of these sections are divided into subsectoins

We would argue that it is best to understand these sections as the angualr rotatoins of a clock. This clock represents many things. First of all is is a series of shifting perspectives on and adumbrations of a singular space-time moment, what Levinas refers to as 'the chronological order that precedes the logical order. Second, it can be understood as marking out different hours of the day, with the first section on 'interioirty' discussing the morning and the second section on 'exterioiry' as discussing the evening. . Understading the most important clock reuires us first to investiate the not just the structurebut the story being narrated by TOtality and Infinity
